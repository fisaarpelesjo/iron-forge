# Zercher Squat And EMG Activity Across Squat Variations

## Source

**Title:** Evaluation of Muscle Activities During Different Squat Variations Using Electromyography Signals

**Authors:** Erdag Deniz and Yavuz Hasan Ulas

**Publication context:** ICSCCW 2019, Advances in Intelligent Systems and Computing, volume 1095, Springer Nature Switzerland AG, 2020.

**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-35249-3_114

**PDF used:** `temp/481168_1_En_114_Chapter_OnlinePDF.PDF`

## Why This Matters For IronForge

IronForge currently uses **Zercher squat 3x5** as the first active lower-body exercise because the current home setup does not have a proper squat rack.

This paper is useful because it compares Zercher squat with other squat variations using EMG measurements. It does not prove that Zercher squat is "better" than back squat, but it gives practical context for what muscles are active and how Zercher compares to other squat patterns.

## Study Question

The study compared muscle activity during five squat variations:

- Back squat
- Front squat
- Hack squat
- Sumo squat
- Zercher squat

The main question was how muscle activation differs across these squat styles when each is performed at the same relative intensity.

## Participants

The study used:

- 14 healthy males
- mean age: about 23.7 years
- average squat training experience: about 3.2 years
- no orthopedic injuries that would affect squat performance

All participants had experience with squatting.

## Muscles Measured

The study measured EMG activity in:

- Rectus femoris
- Vastus medialis
- Vastus lateralis
- Gluteus maximus
- Semitendinosus
- Biceps femoris
- Erector spinae

These cover the main knee extensors, hip extensors, hamstrings, and spinal stabilizers relevant to squatting.

## Protocol

The researchers tested each participant's 1RM for each squat variation.

During the EMG testing session:

- each squat variation was performed for 6 repetitions
- load was set to 60% of that variation's own 1RM
- the variation order was randomized
- analysis used repetitions 2 through 5
- rest between sets was 10 minutes

This matters because each lift was compared at the same relative intensity, not the same absolute load.

## Absolute Loads Used

The paper reported that the back squat used the highest absolute load, while Zercher squat used one of the lower loads.

Approximate mean loads at 60% of each variation's 1RM:

| Variation | Mean Load |
|---|---:|
| Back squat | 103.5 kg |
| Sumo squat | 91.5 kg |
| Hack squat | 80.1 kg |
| Front squat | 79.7 kg |
| Zercher squat | 74.1 kg |

Practical takeaway: it is normal for Zercher squat load to be lower than back squat load.

## EMG Results

Mean EMG activity was reported as percentage of MVIC.

| Muscle | Back Squat | Front Squat | Hack Squat | Sumo Squat | Zercher Squat |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Rectus femoris | 31.8 | 36.1 | 32.1 | 28.7 | 29.2 |
| Vastus medialis | 59.5 | 61.6 | 56.1 | 59.6 | 54.9 |
| Vastus lateralis | 56.3 | 58.4 | 52.1 | 56.5 | 51.8 |
| Erector spinae | 41.9 | 36.8 | 20.9 | 39.2 | 37.8 |
| Gluteus maximus | 19.5 | 18.3 | 14.2 | 18.3 | 18.1 |
| Biceps femoris | 7.1 | 6.8 | 6.5 | 7.1 | 7.3 |
| Semitendinosus | 10.1 | 8.2 | 5.1 | 9.4 | 9.9 |

## Main Findings

### Quadriceps

Front squat produced the highest rectus femoris, vastus medialis, and vastus lateralis activity.

Zercher squat showed the lowest mean quadriceps activity among the tested variations, and the front squat was significantly higher than Zercher squat for rectus femoris.

Practical meaning: Zercher squat still trains the quadriceps, but this study does not support it as the best quadriceps-focused squat variation compared with front squat.

### Glutes

The study found no significant difference between squat variations for gluteus maximus activity.

Practical meaning: Zercher squat can still be treated as a compound lower-body movement, but glute activity may depend heavily on squat depth, load, and individual technique.

### Hamstrings

Biceps femoris activity did not significantly differ across variations.

Semitendinosus activity was lower in the hack squat compared with the other variations. Zercher squat was similar to back squat and sumo squat for semitendinosus in the reported means.

Practical meaning: Zercher squat does not appear to remove hamstring involvement, but it is not primarily a hamstring exercise.

### Erector Spinae

Hack squat had notably lower erector spinae activity than the other variations.

Zercher squat showed meaningful erector spinae activity, close to front squat and sumo squat in the reported means.

Practical meaning: Zercher squat still demands trunk and spinal stabilization. It should be loaded conservatively at first, especially in a home setup without a rack.

## What This Means For Programming

For IronForge, keeping Zercher squat as **3x5** is reasonable.

Reasons:

- the exercise is new relative to the previous back squat pattern
- the absolute load should be expected to be lower
- the lift has meaningful trunk demand
- the bar position can limit performance before the legs do
- fewer reps make technique easier to control while learning

Recommended starting approach:

```text
Zercher squat
3 sets x 5 reps
RPE 6-7 at first
start around 25-30 kg
increase only if elbow position, trunk position, and depth remain controlled
```

## Practical Coaching Notes

For a home gym without a squat rack:

- Zercher squat is a practical replacement because the bar can start lower.
- It should not be compared directly to previous back squat loads.
- The bar should sit in the crook of the elbows.
- Use a towel, pad, or thick shirt if the elbow crease is irritated.
- Keep the torso braced.
- Keep the load light enough that the limiting factor is not elbow pain.
- Treat it as a new lift with its own progression history.

## What The Paper Does Not Prove

The study does not prove:

- that Zercher squat is safer than back squat
- that Zercher squat is better for hypertrophy
- that Zercher squat is better for strength
- that the same results apply to all lifters
- that Zercher squat is ideal for people with elbow pain

It only compares EMG activity across squat variations under the study's conditions.

## Limitations

Important limitations:

- small sample size
- only healthy young male participants
- only EMG was measured
- kinematics were not evaluated
- loads were relative to each variation's own 1RM
- results may differ with different depths, stances, loads, or experience levels

The paper itself notes that kinematic analysis would be needed to support the findings more fully.

## IronForge Takeaway

For this project, the article supports a conservative interpretation:

```text
Zercher squat is a legitimate squat variation with lower expected absolute load,
meaningful trunk demand, and enough lower-body involvement to use as the current
rack-free squat replacement.
```

The current plan remains:

```text
Zercher squat - 3x5
```

Historical `Agachamento (barra)` logs should remain historical data. New Zercher squat logs should build their own progression history.
